The Design Escape

Beginning at Patina means a relaxed, no-fuss speedboat arrival and immediate immersion in Fari Islands' design and dining scene. You then finish somewhere quieter and more secluded at Kanuhura — the trip winds down rather than up.

Patina is the Maldives at its most contemporary — sculptural architecture, an art-forward marina and one of the strongest dining collections in the country. Kanuhura answers with barefoot Six Senses soul: long beaches, a laid-back rhythm and wellness woven through the day.

Because both resorts sit under one brand, the inter-resort seaplane is complimentary — roughly $1,000 of value for two — and the entire trip is handled by a single team from arrival to departure.

Kuda Huraa is the polished, family-run classic close to Malé; Landaa is the grand, nature-rich flagship in Baa Atoll — home to a marine research centre and a short boat ride from Hanifaru Bay, one of the world's great manta and whale-shark sites. Together they are the most complete, worry-free Four Seasons experience.

Banyan Tree is close to Malé, so you're on the sand within half an hour of landing — no seaplane on day one. You then move to the larger Kanuhura for more variety in the second half.

Vabbinfaru is a small, walk-around-in-minutes coral island with an exceptional house reef right off the beach — turtles, reef sharks and rays without a boat. It's intimate and unhurried. Kanuhura then opens things up: more restaurants, a longer beach and more activities to fill the days.
