Getting Around

Renting a car is the best way to experience Paros. One of the island's greatest strengths is its scale. It's big enough to offer distinct villages and beaches, yet compact enough that everything feels within reach. You'll spend less time in the car and more time enjoying the island. I also coordinate private boat transfers and private helicopter transfers, through my partnership with Hoper, for travelers moving between Paros and nearby islands like Sifnos and Milos.

Keith's Take

Paros is the island I find myself recommending more every year. It has beautiful beaches, exceptional restaurants, stylish hotels, and authentic villages without feeling overly polished. If you're looking for the Cyclades beyond the postcard version of Greece, this is where I'd start.

Parīlio

Paros' design icon

Parīlio helped redefine luxury on Paros. Contemporary Cycladic architecture, calming interiors, and exceptional hospitality create a hotel that feels effortlessly sophisticated. Just minutes from Naousa, it's the perfect balance between peaceful retreat and vibrant island life.

Best For Design Lovers, Couples, Wellness, Quiet Luxury
Starting Rate From $900/night
Room I'd Book Sun Suite with Private PoolA spacious indoor-outdoor retreat that perfectly captures Parīlio's relaxed design philosophy.
Keith's Notes

Reserve a pool suite. The generous outdoor space is part of what makes Parīlio so memorable.

Enjoy a beach day at Cosme. Parīlio guests receive complimentary sunbeds at its sister property just outside Naousa.

Pair quiet mornings with lively evenings. Spend your days at the hotel before heading into Naousa for dinner.

Keith's Take

Parīlio is one of those hotels that's easy to recommend. It captures everything people love about Paros: relaxed luxury, beautiful design, and an atmosphere that never feels overly polished or performative.

Andronis Minois

A modern island escape

Andronis Minois offers a more relaxed side of Paros. Tucked away from the island's busiest villages, it combines generous suites, thoughtful service, and a peaceful atmosphere that's ideal for travelers seeking space to truly unwind.

Best For Families, Couples, Longer Stays, Resort Lovers
Starting Rate From $700/night
Room I'd Book Minois Blu Suite Sea ViewOne of the property's most spacious accommodations, with panoramic sea views and room to truly settle in.
Keith's Notes

Stay for at least four nights. The slower pace rewards travelers who aren't in a hurry.

Take advantage of the complimentary shuttle. It's an easy way to enjoy Naousa for dinner without worrying about parking or driving home.

Explore Aliki. The charming fishing village is just minutes away and one of my favorite spots for a leisurely seafood lunch by the water.

Keith's Take

For travelers who prioritize space, comfort, and a true resort atmosphere, Andronis Minois delivers one of the island's most relaxing luxury stays.

Paros

Cosme

Naousa at your doorstep

Many luxury hotels in Paros ask you to choose between staying on the beach or staying near Naousa. Cosme gives you both. Just outside town, it combines a true beachfront setting with easy walking access to some of the island's best restaurants, bars, and boutiques.

Best For Beach Lovers, Couples, Walkability, Luxury Travelers
Starting Rate From $950/night
Room I'd Book Cosmos SuiteThe standout choice for travelers who want Cosme at its very best.
Keith's Notes

Don't overlook the entry-level rooms. They're among the largest standard accommodations I toured anywhere on Paros, making the starting rate easier to justify.

Choose your suite based on your travel style. The Cosmos Suite is the hotel's showpiece, but I actually preferred the Enastro Suite for travelers who value a little more privacy.

Walk into Naousa for dinner. One of Cosme's biggest advantages is being able to leave the car behind and enjoy the town on foot.

Keith's Take

Cosme solves one of Paros' biggest compromises. For travelers who want the energy of Naousa without giving up the feel of a true beachfront resort, it's one of the smartest choices on Paros.

Paros · Three Perfect Days

How I'd Spend Three Days Here

How I'd spend three unforgettable days on one of Greece's most effortlessly beautiful islands.

Day One | Arrive

Ease into Paros with an afternoon at your hotel before heading into Naousa as the sun begins to set. Browse the boutiques, wander the harbor, and settle in for a leisurely dinner at Siparos while the waterfront comes alive after dark.

Stay: Parilio
Dinner: Siparos

Day Two | Discover

Start the morning exploring the mountain village of Lefkes, then continue south to Aliki for lunch at To Balconi tou Aki, one of my favorite seaside tavernas on the island. Spend the afternoon swimming at the crystal-clear waters of Lageri Beach before returning to Lefkes for dinner at Santa Pacou Restaurant.

Don't Miss: Sunset wandering the marble streets of Lefkes.

Day Three | Unwind

Ease into the morning before spending the day at Crios Beach Club, where you'll find one of the island's most relaxed beachfront settings for swimming, lunch, and an afternoon by the sea. End your stay with dinner at Parostia at Cosme, one of my favorite restaurants on the island and the perfect way to close out a trip to Paros.

Keith's Tip

One of Paros' greatest strengths is its scale. The island is compact enough that you can enjoy a leisurely beach day without feeling like you're missing out. Slow down and let each part of the island unfold at its own pace.
